# EB-2 NIW Research Narrative – ASTRA

## Overview

ASTRA is an AI-enabled personal safety system designed to improve protection for vulnerable users through a hybrid safety architecture that combines:

- manual emergency alerting
- contextual risk awareness
- behavioral anomaly detection
- adaptive escalation support
- trusted-contact coordination

This project is being developed as both a deployable technology platform and a research-driven safety initiative.

---

## Proposed Endeavor

My proposed endeavor is to develop AI-enabled personal safety technologies that improve real-world protection for vulnerable users in the United States.

This work focuses on systems that do not rely only on manual panic activation, but instead combine emergency-response tools with intelligent, context-aware safety support. ASTRA represents this effort through a mobile-first platform that integrates emergency workflows, behavioral risk interpretation, and AI-assisted escalation logic.

---

## Problem

Most existing safety applications are primarily reactive. They are useful when a user can access a device, recognize danger, and manually trigger an alert. However, in many real-world situations, individuals may be unable to do so because of:

- fear
- coercion
- surveillance
- immobilization
- confusion
- sudden distress

In addition, safety risk may build gradually through warning signs such as:

- missed check-ins
- route deviation
- prolonged inactivity
- unusual behavior patterns
- unsafe contextual conditions

This creates a gap in current personal safety systems and motivates the need for more adaptive, AI-assisted solutions.

---

## ASTRA Solution

ASTRA is designed as a hybrid personal safety platform that extends manual emergency tools rather than replacing them.

Core system capabilities include:

- panic alert workflows
- real-time location sharing
- trusted-contact notification
- missed check-in monitoring
- contextual safety awareness
- behavioral anomaly detection
- dynamic risk scoring
- evidence capture and secure storage
- adaptive emergency escalation

If a user manually activates an emergency alert, ASTRA supports immediate response workflows. If the user is unable to act, the platform is designed to interpret contextual and behavioral indicators of elevated concern and support earlier escalation.

---

## Technical Approach

ASTRA is structured as a modular safety platform with multiple system layers:

- **mobile interaction layer** for panic alerts, check-ins, and user-facing safety workflows
- **backend infrastructure layer** for secure data processing, user management, and alert orchestration
- **risk assessment layer** for contextual scoring, anomaly detection, and decision logic
- **communication layer** for trusted-contact notification, chat, SMS fallback, and escalation support
- **evidence subsystem** for emergency audio, video, photo, timestamp, and GPS-linked records

The AI component is designed to evolve in stages. Early system logic uses rule-based and contextual scoring approaches. Future work is intended to expand toward machine-learning-based anomaly detection, predictive risk modeling, and more advanced behavioral interpretation.

---

## Innovation

ASTRA is innovative because it shifts personal safety systems in several important ways:

- from **reactive** response to **proactive** support
- from **manual-only activation** to **hybrid human-AI coordination**
- from **isolated alerts** to **continuous contextual interpretation**
- from **single emergency triggers** to **multi-layer escalation workflows**

This makes ASTRA more than a panic button app. It is a broader safety intelligence framework designed for real-world risk detection and emergency support.
